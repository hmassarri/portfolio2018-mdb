Process

 

Mental Model

Capture user behavior, pain points, motivations, and needs from contextual inquiries. Group into logical pillars. Map out product features for each pillar to identify gaps, opportunities, and feature bloat.

 

Personas

Summarize the research data from your mental model into personas. This will help your team keep the user’s motivations, frustrrations, and goals in mind when evaluate design solutions.

 

Customer Journey Map

After you have identified who will be using your product, write down the business goals/desired outcome for each persona. You then go back and fill in how they will discover your product, learn what it is all about, and how they will start using it.

 

Task Analysis

Identifies user’s thought process and desired reactions toward each task flow segment.

 

Red Route Analysis

Prioritize user experience for common tasks. Tasks used by few and rarely can be harder for user to access so that tasks used frequently by most people can be easily accomplished.

 

Storyboards

Visually depict scenarios with storyboards. The storyboard should show the context, the problem, the user’s fears/motiviations, and how the problem will be solved.

 

Wireframes

Visually communicate abstract concepts with users and team members to generate feedback. These can be drawn by hand or with software like Sketch, Balsamiq, Axure, or Omnigraffle

 

Rapid Prototyping

Get users involved early in the design process with quick and inexpensive paper prototypes. Uncover usability issues and validate design decisions based on acutal user reactions.

 

Style Tiles

Jumpstart the visual design language even before the interaction designs are finalized. Start with common UI elements or components that would be found in the product your are building and iterate through several varations.

 

High Fidelity Mockups and Prototypes

Apply product branding, grid structure, typography, color scheme, and icons. Develop interactive prototypes with tools like Sketch or Invision. This lets the team easily observe exactly how well the product will be received before the product gets built. It gives the team a chance to see and take time to identify any roadblocks that haven’t been surfaced yet.

 

Pattern Library

Create a reusable pattern library in an app like Sketch so that you and your team member do not have to keep recreating commonly used controls over and over again in your designs.
