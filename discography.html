BLACK TRANSMITTER (2016 - Present)

"INIT Signal: Return EP" / Skunkworks Records
Listen

My current band. Tim (drums) and I had worked on a few projects just making noise and learning how to create “mood” on audio. That was the goal that we weren’t even sure how to acheive or if we even could. I had some new material in the same style of surf / punk / sci-fi which needed some work and we decided to do this band. Juan (bass) joined about a year later and we haven’t looked back. We are currently working on our LP due to be released in 2019.

theUNIDENTIFIED (2000 - 2005)

"Secret Audio Suspense LP" released on Skunkworks Records
Listen

The second lineup (after Steve Infinity, Poznanski, and Loni Activator had left to pursue other bands / endeavors) was The Agent (drums) / Johnny Sonic (keyboards, however he left to start his band "Fun Machine" before this recording happened) / and Recon Rob (bass). This was the last recording the band did before disbanding in 2005. The last show was at the Asbury Lanes in Nov 2005 with Tokyo Rose on drums.

theUNIDENTIFIED (2000 - 2005)

"00.02 EP" released on Skunkworks Records
Listen

So after a 4 year break, I decided it was time to start my own band with the music that I had been writing on the side that lent itself to a sound between surf, punk, new-wave, and rock. Starting as a three piece, we: Steve Infinity (bass), Poznanski (drums) and me (guitar / vocals) became “theUNIDENTIFIED”. We chose that name because couldn’t nail down exactly the style of music we were performing and the band name choices were totally awful. A year later, "Loni Activator" (keyboards / vocals) joined the band. This was the original lineup.

EYE FED FINK / CLEVER HANS (1996)

“CD LP” released on Random Wood Vol. 1 Comp, Iguana Records
Listen on NJPP Archives

2 bands. 2 songs. 1 comp. 3/4 of the Missing Children: Mark (bass), Alex (drums), me (guitar). Eye Fed Fink was the start of going down the surf rock route with some edge. Clever Hans was the start of us going down a more musically challenging route heavily inspired by No Means No. After this comp both bands broke up.

MISSING CHILDREN (1994 - 1995)

“S/T 7" EP" released on Craig Records
Listen

This is the first band I played in at 19. We released this one and only EP. This was a defining moment for that is when I was introduced to the true meaning and lessons of collaboration and how to craft art from it. I was also now part of something bigger than myself, and making some loud fast music. All things come to an end, so we broke up. Frank (vocals) and our record label / band manager (Craig) went on to form the Degenerics. This band was the right mix of time and place and people and I was forturnate to be a part of it.
