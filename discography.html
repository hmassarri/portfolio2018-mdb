Discography

 

BLACK TRANSMITTER (2016 - Present)

"INIT Signal: Return EP" released on Skunkworks Records

My current band. Tim (drums) and I had worked on a few projects just making noise and learning how to create “mood” on audio. That was goal that we weren’t even sure how to acheive or if we even could. I had some songs I had been writing, in the same style of surf / punk / sci-fi which needed some work and we decided to do this band. Juan (bass) joined about a year later and we haven’t looked back. We are currently working on our LP due to be released in 2019.

blacktransmitter.bandcamp.com

theUNIDENTIFIED (2000 - 2005)

"Secret Audio Suspense LP" released on Skunkworks Records

The second wave of the band (after Steve Infinity, Poznanski, and Loni Activator) was The Agent (drums) / Johnny Sonic (keyboards, however, he left shortly after to start the group Fun Machine before this recording happened) / and Recon Rob (bass). This was the last recording the band did before disbanding in 2005. The last show was at the Asbury Lanes in Nov 2005 with Tokyo Rose on drums.

theunidentified.bandcamp.com

theUNIDENTIFIED (2000 - 2005)

"00.02 EP" released on Skunkworks Records

After joining and playing with a numerous bands that never recorded anything, I decided it was time to start my own band with the music that I had been writing on the side then lent itself to somewhere between surf / punk / sci-fi / and rock. Starting as a three piece, we: Steve Infinity (bass), Poznanski (drums) and me (guitar / vocals) became “theUNIDENTIFIED” due to the fact we couldn’t nail down exactly the style of music we were performing and because the band name choices were totally awful. Eventually there would be a fourth member to the band, "Loni Activator" who contributed to keyboards and vocals. This was the first wave of the band

theunidentified.bandcamp.com

EYE FED FINK / CLEVER HANS (1996)

“CD LP” released on Random Wood Vol. 1 Comp, Iguana Records

2 bands. 2 songs. 1 comp. 3/4 of the Missing Children: Mark (bass), Alex (drums), me (guitar). Eye Fed Fink was the start of going down the surf rock route with some edge. Clever Hans was the start of us going down a more musically challenging route heavily inspired by No Means No. After this comp both bands broke up.

Listen to both tracks here on the NJPP Archives

MISSING CHILDREN (1994 - 1995)

“S/T 7" EP" released on Craig Records

This is the first band I played in at 19. We released this one and only EP. This was a defining moment for that is when I was introduced to the true meaning and lessons of collaboration and how to craft art from it. I was also now part of something bigger than myself, and making some loud fast music. All things come to an end, so we broke up. Frank (vocals) and our record label / band manager (Craig) went on to form the Degenerics, one of the seminole punk / hardcore bands that came out of New Brunswick NJ, who carved out a name for themselves with their incredible contribution of their music and words to a dying scene. I'm just fortunate to have been part of the band that started lifetime friendships, creativity, and inspiration for many.

missingchildren.bandcamp.com
